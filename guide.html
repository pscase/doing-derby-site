Welcome to the World of the Kentucky Derby

In Louisville, preparations for the Kentucky Derby begin almost as soon as the Garland of Roses is draped across the winning horse each May. Before the crowd has even left Churchill Downs, race fans are already imagining the following year—planning trips, reserving tables, and perhaps dreaming up an even more spectacular Derby hat.

But when early spring arrives, Louisville begins to hum with a different kind of energy. Visitors start to trickle into the city. Shop windows fill with pastel dresses and seersucker suits. Restaurants begin taking reservations weeks in advance. The anticipation of Derby week is no longer a distant idea—it is suddenly just around the corner.

The Kentucky Derby is often called “the most exciting two minutes in sports,” but anyone who has experienced it knows the truth: the Derby is not simply a race. It is a week-long celebration of racing, style, tradition, and unmistakable Southern hospitality that transforms Louisville each spring.

Derby week unfolds across the city in a whirl of celebrations—galas, concerts, bourbon tastings, charity events, and beloved traditions like the Kentucky Oaks on the Friday before Derby Day. Hotels fill months in advance. Bourbon flows freely. And on the first Saturday in May, thousands gather beneath the twin spires of Churchill Downs wearing elaborate hats, bright spring dresses, and crisp linen suits, all ready to witness a tradition that has been part of Kentucky culture for more than a century.

But the richness of Kentucky’s racing culture extends well beyond Louisville.

Just an hour to the east, in the rolling bluegrass hills surrounding Lexington, the spring meet at Keeneland Race Course is an experience all its own. Each April, Keeneland’s grandstand fills with racing fans, families, and tailgaters enjoying one of the most beautiful racecourses in the world. Keeneland is not a warmup for anything — it is where thoroughbred racing feels most organic, most authentically Kentuckian. The Spring meet wraps up the week before the first Saturday in May, and for those who experience it, Keeneland becomes a cherished tradition in its own right.

As the Keeneland meet draws to a close, anticipation shifts west toward Derby week. Horses arrive from across the country. Trainers and jockeys prepare for the biggest race of their careers. And visitors begin arriving in Kentucky ready to experience the Derby for themselves.

This guide is designed to help you do exactly that.

Whether it is your first Derby or your tenth, whether you plan to spend your weekend beneath the twin spires or exploring horse farms and bourbon distilleries across the Bluegrass, the following pages will help you experience Kentucky the way insiders do.

Because once you experience the Kentucky Derby, you quickly learn something locals have always known: The race may last only two minutes—but the magic of Derby week lasts much longer.

Derby Tip

Arrive early on Derby Day. Churchill Downs opens hours before the big race, and the early morning atmosphere is one of the most memorable parts of the experience.

    From historic downtown hotels to charming neighborhoods and smart alternatives, a guide to finding the right base for your Derby weekend.

  7. Getting Tickets to the Kentucky Derby

  8. Kentucky Oaks Day

    The Friday before the Derby—often called the locals’ favorite day at Churchill Downs. Why seasoned Derby-goers consider Oaks Day the hidden gem of the weekend.

  9. What to Wear to the Derby

  14. Doing Derby Like a Local
