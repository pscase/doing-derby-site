Keeneland & Lexington

The Heart of Bluegrass Racing — Where Horsemen Feel Most at Home

The Jewel of the Bluegrass

Ask any seasoned horseman their favorite racetrack in America, and more often than not the answer is Keeneland. Nestled on the outskirts of Lexington, Kentucky, Keeneland Race Course is where the sport of thoroughbred racing feels closest to its roots — intimate, beautiful, and steeped in tradition.

Unlike the spectacle of Churchill Downs on Derby Day, Keeneland offers something different: a quieter elegance, stone walls wrapped in ivy, the sound of hooves on limestone-rich soil, and a crowd that genuinely knows and loves the horses. The spring meet runs through April, with the final races wrapping up just before the first Saturday in May. It is an essential experience in its own right — the most organic, authentically Kentuckian racing you will find anywhere.

What Makes Keeneland Special

  • No announcer during races — At Keeneland, the races unfold in a rare, atmospheric silence. You hear the crowd, the thunder of hooves, and nothing else. It's an experience unlike any other track in the world.
  • The Keeneland breakfast — Arrive early for the famed trackside breakfast, where you can watch morning workouts while enjoying biscuits, country ham, and coffee in the crisp Bluegrass air.
  • The paddock experience — Keeneland's paddock is one of the most beautiful in racing. Walk right up to the horses before each race and see these incredible athletes up close.
  • Accessible and intimate — General admission is affordable, the grounds are walkable, and the atmosphere is welcoming to newcomers and lifelong fans alike.

Lexington: The Horse Capital of the World

Lexington is a city that wears its identity with quiet pride. The University of Kentucky gives it youthful energy, while the surrounding horse farms give it timeless beauty. Downtown Lexington has blossomed into a destination for food lovers, with farm-to-table restaurants, craft cocktail bars, and a walkable core that makes it easy to explore on foot.

Beyond the restaurants, Lexington is the gateway to Kentucky's horse country. Within a twenty-minute drive, you can visit world-famous farms like Claiborne, Three Chimneys, and Taylor Made, where Derby winners and legends of the turf are born and raised. Many farms offer guided tours — a rare chance to walk among thoroughbreds on some of the most beautiful land in America.

Doing Derby's Keeneland Tips

  • Arrive early for the trackside breakfast — it fills up fast, and the morning light over the track is extraordinary.
  • Dress smart but comfortable. Keeneland is more relaxed than Derby Day, but the crowd still takes style seriously.
  • Don't miss the Keeneland gift shop — it has some of the best equestrian and racing-themed merchandise anywhere.
  • If you're visiting during the spring meet, plan to spend a full day. The races run from early afternoon, but the experience starts in the morning.
  • Book a horse farm tour through Visit Horse Country — they coordinate tours at many of the region's top farms.
